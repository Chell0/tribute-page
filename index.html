Thomas J.O. "Tom" Mboya

The greatest president Kenya never had.

From left: John F. Kennedy with Thomas J.O. Mboya in the United States in 1960.

Here's a timeline of Thomas J.O. Mboya's life:

  • 1930 - Born in Kilima Mbogo, Kenya.
  • 1948 - Attends Royal Sanitary Institute's Medical Training School for Sanitary Inspectors in Nairobi.
  • 1950 - Qualifies as an Inspector.
  • 1955 - Receives scholarship from Britain's Trade Union Congress to attend Ruskin College in Oxford, where he studied industrial management.
  • 1956 - Graduates from college and returns back to Kenya to join politics.
  • 1957 - Forms his own party, the People's Congress Party (PCP).
  • 1958 - Elected as the conference chariman of African People's Conference (APC) in Ghana.
  • 1959 - Organizes the Airlift Africa Project (AAP) together with the African-American Students Foundation (AASF) in the United States through which 81 Kenyan students were flown to the U.S to study in the United States uiversities including Barack Obama's father, Barack Obama Sr.
  • 1962 - Marries wife of 7 years Pamela Odede, and together are blessed with 5 children.
  • 1963 - Elected as member of parliament and Minister of Justice & Constitutional Affairs and later bcame Minister of Economic Planning & Development.
  • 1964 - Negotiates with the UK & colonial government to set conditions for Kenya's independence.
  • 1969 - Dies at the age of 39 years.

"Kenya's Independence would have been seriously compromised were it not for the courage and steadfastness of Tom Mboya."

Kenya's First President Jomo Kenyatta
